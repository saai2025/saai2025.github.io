Amir Roushdy

Associate Professor of Mechatronics & Robotics at German University in Cairo, Cairo, Egypt

German University in Cairo, Cairo, Egypt

Biography

Assoc. Prof. Amir Roushdy has been an Associate Professor of Mechatronics Engineering at the German University in Cairo (GUC) since 2022. His research focuses on advanced robotics and mechatronic systems, integrating micro-optical sensors, digital twins, and whispering gallery mode (WGM) phenomena for high-precision sensing and control applications. He received his B.Sc. in Mechatronics, followed by an M.Sc. in Mechatronics from the German University in Cairo (GUC), and later earned a Ph.D. from Southern Methodist University (SMU), Dallas, Texas, USA, specializing in micro-optical sensor technologies and their applications in advanced robotics and mechatronic systems. Since 2015, he has been a faculty member in the Mechatronics Engineering Department at GUC. His research is centered on integrating micro-resonator-based opto-mechanical sensors, digital twin technology, and AI-driven automation into robotic and mechatronic systems to enhance perception, control, and interaction. His work has contributed to the development of high-precision optical sensing solutions for humanoid robots, telepresence robots, and AI-assisted robotic platforms.

Prof. Roushdy is the Founder and Head of the ARAtronics Research Center at the GUC, a leading hub for robotics, AI, digital twins, and mechatronics research. His team specializes in designing and developing next-generation robotic systems, including humanoid robots, telepresence robots, and AI-driven automation solutions. Under his leadership, the ARAtronics team has contributed to multiple industry collaborations and international conferences, pushing the boundaries of robotics, digital twins, and optical sensor integration. In addition to his academic leadership, Prof. Roushdy leads ARAtech Innovations, an industry-focused group dedicated to transforming research and development into market-ready products. Through this initiative, he drives the implementation of cutting-edge technologies in robotics, AI, digital twins, and optical sensing for real-world applications.

He has authored numerous peer-reviewed journal articles and conference papers in the fields of robotics, mechatronics, optical sensor technologies, and digital twins. His research contributions have been recognized with multiple awards, including the Best Research Award across the Research Day at SMU, Texas, in 2013. In 2017, he was recognized as a Notable Alumni of SMU, Dallas, Texas, USA, for his contributions to robotics and optical sensing technologies. Prof. Roushdy is a member of the Society of Photo-optical Instrumentation Engineers (SPIE), the American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME), and the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA). He has served as a reviewer for several high-impact journals and as a technical committee member for international conferences in robotics, mechatronics, digital twins, and optical sensing technologies.
