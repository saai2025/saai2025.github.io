Summer School on Affordable AI


Welcome/Willkommen (de)/أهلاً وسهلاً (ar) to the 2nd Edition of the Summer School on Affordable AI (SAAI – سعي) 2025. This initiative is part of a collaborative project where academia meets innovation, fostering global collaboration and intellectual excellence. Organized by the Albarqouni Lab at the University Hospital Bonn, University of Bonn, and Helmholtz AI in Germany, in partnership with the German University in Cairo in Egypt, this program offers a transformative educational experience. Through engaging lectures, hands-on labs, and real-world challenges, participants explore cutting-edge advancements in AI across multiple disciplines, guided by expert faculty and skilled tutors.

Register (Stay Tuned)

Join us as we embark on this journey between 13th and 17th Sep. in Cairo, Egypt to shape the future of AI, making it accessible and affordable for all. The deadline for regsitartion is 15th May 2025 at 10AM CEST.

SAAI’s Mission

Our mission revolves around three core pillars

  • Innovative Teaching Modules: We are dedicated to crafting teaching course modules that are not only informed by the latest AI developments but also finely tuned to the specific needs and resource limitations of low-income countries in the MENA region. The modules equip participants with essential AI skills to address common challenges in Germany and Arab nations effectively.
  • Knowledge Transfer and Research Excellence: With participation from diverse academic backgrounds, we foster knowledge transfer and collaborative learning among approximately 30 students and graduates, and 12 faculty members from Germany and Arab nations.

Interdicplinary project

Cost-Effective Affordable AI for Breast Lesion Detection with Ultra-sensitive Optical Sensing

Breast cancer is a leading global health challenge, with over 2.3 million new cases and 685,000 deaths in 2020. Early and accurate detection is critical but remains inaccessible in low-resource settings due to the high cost and infrastructure demands of traditional diagnostic methods like mammography and digital pathology. This project aims to address these barriers by developing a novel, cost-effective diagnostic tool that combines biosensor technology with advanced Machine Learning (ML) and Deep Learning algorithms. Through international collaboration between the GUC ARAtronics Research Center and the Albarqouni Lab at the University of Bonn, the project seeks to create affordable, accurate, and scalable solutions, contributing to broader health equity and advancing AI-driven diagnostics for low-resource environments.

Albarqouni Lab

University Hospital Bonn, University of Bonn and Helmholtz AI

Albarqouni Lab, established in 2020 and located at the Clinic for Diagnostic and Interventional Radiology, University Hospital Bonn, University of Bonn and Helmholtz AI, aims to develop the next generation of AI in Medicine, specifically computational imaging algorithms with a particular focus on artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) models to improve the clinical workflow for patients, radiologists, and healthcare professionals. The lab is affiliated with the Munich School for Data Science (MUDS), the Medical Imaging Centre Bonn (MIB), and the European Laboratory for Learning and Intelligent Systems (ELLIS). The research at the Albarqouni Lab focuses on three main areas: computational medical imaging, federated learning in healthcare, and affordable AI and healthcare.
